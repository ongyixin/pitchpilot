SECTION 1: APPROVED MESSAGING GUIDELINES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1.1 Sales representatives MUST use only approved claims from the current
    messaging guide when presenting to prospects or customers.

1.2 All quantitative claims (speed improvements, cost savings, accuracy rates)
    MUST be accompanied by methodology and sample size. Claims without
    supporting data must not be presented as established facts.

1.3 Comparative claims against named competitors require explicit written
    approval from the Marketing Legal team before use.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
SECTION 2: AUTOMATION AND WORKFLOW CLAIMS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

2.1 PROHIBITED: Representatives may NOT state the product is "fully automated"
    or requires "no manual steps" without qualification.

2.2 REQUIRED QUALIFIER: Any automation claim must acknowledge that:
    - Edge cases and transactions above $10,000 in value require manual review
    - Regulatory-flagged items are automatically queued for human approval
    - The system augments human judgment; it does not replace it

2.3 Approved phrasing: "Our platform automates standard workflows, with
    automatic flagging of exceptions for your team's review."

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
SECTION 3: DATA PRIVACY AND SECURITY CLAIMS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3.1 GDPR COMPLIANCE: The product is designed to support GDPR-compatible
    deployments. Representatives must not state the product is "GDPR-compliant"
    without clarifying that compliance depends on the customer's own configuration,
    deployment context, and data processing obligations.

3.2 REQUIRED: A Data Processing Addendum (DPA) must be offered to any EU
    customer or prospect. The DPA template is available from Legal.

3.3 ON-DEVICE CLAIMS: If the product offers on-device processing as an option,
    representatives must clarify that cloud fallback features, if available,
    are opt-in and disabled by default. Claims of absolute data locality
    must not be made if any cloud component exists anywhere in the product.

3.4 PROHIBITED: Claims of "zero data retention", "no data ever leaves",
    "completely private" require verification by the Security team before use.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
SECTION 4: PERFORMANCE AND ACCURACY CLAIMS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

4.1 Accuracy rates stated in sales presentations must match the most recent
    validated evaluation report. Current approved figures:
    - English-language claim extraction: 91% (±3%) on internal benchmark
    - Multi-language support: accuracy varies; do not cite a single global figure

4.2 Speed/performance claims require:
    - Hardware specification (CPU/GPU model, RAM)
    - Test dataset description
    - Measurement methodology

4.3 PROHIBITED: "10x faster", "99% accurate", "instant" without qualification.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
SECTION 5: CERTIFICATION AND COMPLIANCE BADGE CLAIMS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

5.1 SOC 2 Type II audit is in progress. Until the audit is complete and
    the report issued, representatives must NOT claim SOC 2 compliance.
    Approved phrasing: "We are currently undergoing SOC 2 Type II audit,
    expected completion Q3 2025."

5.2 ISO 27001 certification is not yet in place. Do not reference it.
