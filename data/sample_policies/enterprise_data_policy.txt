ENTERPRISE DATA HANDLING POLICY
Version 2.4 | Effective January 2026

==============================================================
SECTION 1 — DATA PRIVACY AND REPRESENTATION
==============================================================

§1.1  Accurate Representation of Data Storage
All customer-facing communications, presentations, and sales materials must
accurately represent how customer data is stored, processed, and transmitted.

Prohibited language:
- Absolute claims such as "data never leaves your device" or "fully private"
  unless the product architecture unconditionally guarantees this under ALL
  operating modes, including optional or default-enabled features.

Required qualifiers:
- When on-device processing is available but optional, use: "private by default"
  or "on-device when configured in local mode."
- When any feature transmits data externally, this must be disclosed at first
  mention in any sales context.

§1.2  Data Retention Disclosure
All sales materials must include or reference the standard data retention
schedule. Minimum required disclosures:
- Local recordings are stored only on the user's device.
- Automatic deletion is configurable (default: 30 days).
- No data is transmitted to company servers without explicit user opt-in.


==============================================================
SECTION 2 — AUTOMATION CLAIMS
==============================================================

§2.1  Definition of "Fully Automated"
The company products may not be represented as "fully automated" in any
sales presentation, pitch deck, or demo without qualification.

Standard qualifier: "Automated for standard cases; edge cases are flagged
for human review per company policy."

§3.2  Human Oversight Requirement
Any AI-driven decision or output with a model confidence score exceeding
0.95, or any output that affects financial, legal, or compliance domains,
MUST be routed to a human reviewer before delivery to end customers.

This requirement applies to:
- Compliance assessments
- Risk scoring outputs
- Automated approval decisions
- Any output that a customer could rely on as a final determination

Permitted claim: "AI-assisted with optional human-in-the-loop review."
Prohibited claim: "Fully automated — no manual review required."


==============================================================
SECTION 3 — SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS
==============================================================

§3.1  Standard Enterprise SLA
The standard enterprise tier guarantees:
- 99.5 % monthly uptime
- < 200 ms P95 API response time
- 4-hour critical incident response

§3.2  Premium Enterprise SLA (requires separate addendum)
Available on request for customers with >1000 MAU or enterprise contract
value exceeding $250k ARR:
- 99.9 % monthly uptime
- < 100 ms P95 API response time
- 1-hour critical incident response

IMPORTANT: The 99.9 % SLA figure may only be cited in sales presentations
when the customer has signed the SLA Addendum v2. Quoting 99.9 % uptime
to customers on the standard tier creates contractual liability.


==============================================================
SECTION 4 — COMPETITIVE CLAIMS
==============================================================

§4.1  Benchmark Requirements
Any speed, accuracy, or performance comparison claim must:
1. Name the specific competitor product and version being compared.
2. Reference a publicly reproducible benchmark or internal test methodology.
3. Include the test date (benchmarks older than 12 months require a
   refresh before use in sales materials).

Prohibited: "X times faster than competitors" (unanchored)
Permitted: "X times faster than [Competitor] on [benchmark] (tested MM/YYYY)"


==============================================================
SECTION 5 — REGULATORY COMPLIANCE
==============================================================

§5.1  GDPR Article 22 — Automated Decision-Making
Any product feature that constitutes automated decision-making with legal
or similarly significant effects must include:
- Explicit disclosure to affected data subjects
- A mechanism for human review on request
- Documentation in the privacy policy

§5.2  Disclaimer Requirements
All demos or pitches involving AI-generated assessments must include:
"AI outputs are provided as suggestions for human review and do not
constitute legal, compliance, or professional advice."
